Are you a good fit?

It is hard to answer a question like this here, but here goes a list of challenges you should find yourself excited about:

  • You'll be definitely building an exciting product from scratch, so you need to be excited of working on a greenfield project, with loads of decisions yet to be make. In an early stage like this, you'll need to balance several factors, and watching out to over-engineered solutions. You need to understand the reasons behind every feature request, analyze alternatives, weigh the tradeoffs, and choose the solution with the best cost/benefit ratio. Probably weeks or months later, you'll revisit this feature, and you'll evolve it iteratively, pay back some tech debt and refine the solution with more data and experience in your pocket.
  • You like technology, frameworks and programming languages, but what you like most is to solve real business needs with technology. You're the chef which uses the best tools and ingredients available to produce delightful dishes and experiences. You aren't a knife-sharpener, or an oven engineer that creates cutting-edge technology for other chefs.
  • You're open to a wide range of problems, solutions and tools. The most obvious solution can be the worst one. You always look for the simplest, easiest, fastest way to solve a problem, without creating new ones.
  • You consider yourself a data-driven decision maker. You gather data, analyze it and decide the next move based on the insights.
